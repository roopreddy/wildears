What is WildEars?

WildEars turns your phone or computer into a biodiversity survey tool. Record or upload a wildlife sound, and our AI identifies the species for you. Save your sightings to build a "Species Heard Here" list that you can share with parks, teachers, or scientists.

How does it work?

WildEars uses the BirdNET machine learning model from the Cornell Lab of Ornithology and Chemnitz University of Technology. The AI runs entirely in your browser — your audio never leaves your device.

The model analyzes 3-second audio clips, computes a spectrogram (a visual representation of sound frequencies), and compares it against patterns learned from thousands of recordings.

Honest Limitations

  • Results show likely species, not certainty. Always allow for expert confirmation.
  • Works best for birds. Frogs, bats, and insects are harder to identify and may need a different tool.
  • Needs an internet connection to download the AI model the first time (after that it's cached).
  • Background noise (wind, traffic, multiple species) reduces accuracy.
  • Confidence scores below 50% should be treated as educated guesses.

Tips for Best Results

  • Record in a quiet area, as close to the animal as safely possible.
  • Keep recordings to a single species call if possible.
  • Morning (dawn chorus) is the best time for bird identification.
  • Hold your phone steady — rustling and handling noise can confuse the AI.

About This Project

WildEars is a FIRST LEGO League Innovation Project for the 2026-27 BIOGLOW season. It pairs with our "Listening to the Wild" physical recorder to make biodiversity monitoring accessible to everyone — for free.

Built with input from local wildlife experts in the Sacramento area, including the Effie Yeaw Nature Center and Sacramento Audubon Society.

Sound identification powered by BirdNET (Cornell Lab of Ornithology & Chemnitz University of Technology)
