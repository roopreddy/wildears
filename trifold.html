⚠️ Why This Matters

North America has lost 3 billion birds since 1970. Frog and mammal populations are also declining. Monitoring wildlife is critical — but traditional surveys need experts and expensive gear. WildEars lets anyone contribute.

⚠ Invasive Species: Why It Matters

The story of the European Starling

🐦 European Starling
Sturnus vulgaris
In 1890–91, 100 starlings were released in New York's Central Park by a group wanting every bird mentioned by Shakespeare in America. Today, over 200 million span the continent.
They aggressively outcompete native cavity-nesting birds like bluebirds, woodpeckers, and swallows for nest sites — a major threat to Sacramento's native species.

Impact on Native Wildlife
  • Takes nest cavities from bluebirds, woodpeckers & swallows
  • Spreads diseases to native bird populations

💡 How WildEars Helps

WildEars automatically flags invasive species when detected. Communities can track where starlings are heard, share data with wildlife managers, and support native species through nest box programs.
